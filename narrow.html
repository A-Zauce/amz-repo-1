General Sherman Stopped Too Soon

During the US Civil War, General William Tecumseh Sherman burned Georgia to the ground.

He should have taken his job seriously, made a left turn, and paved a sixty-mile-wide path out of the South.

The fact that he didn't burn all of South Carolina, traitorous as they were, is a tragedy.

His army should have paved a path back to D.C. through Richmond.
